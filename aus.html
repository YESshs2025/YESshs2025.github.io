Uhm what's Undertale

Undertale is an indie RPG created by Toby Fox. Go search it up or something, or here's a list to its Wikipedia page. I'm too lazy to explain it. Plus, I'm trying not to fail a web design class right now. Maybe I'll explain it later when I have time.

The reason Undertale was and still is so popular, the reason the fandom is somehow still here after eight and a half years, is because it really made people feel emotions, and with emotions come creativity and inspiration.

Well then what's an AU

AU stands for alternate universe. Like, for example, if you were on AO3 (fanfiction website) and you came across a fic with the 'Soulmates AU' tag that would mean that this fic takes place in an alternate universe in which, in this case, soulmates are a concept that exists. (wow a totally normal example that normal people will understand)

So an Undertale AU ™ is an alternate universe of Undertale. It's based on Undertale, but with a twist. Sometimes, it's a thematic change that doesn't change that anything much lore-wise, like Outertale being literally just Undertale but it looks ✨aesthetic✨ because space. Other times, the AU is a "what if?" of the original story of Undertale, like in Dusttale and Something New.

In some cases, it almost seems like the creator was originally just doing a thematic change, but as the worldbuilding progressed, potential for story deviations naturally formed out of their circumstances, leading to unique and fitting new stories. This probably happened with Underlust.

Some AUs build upon other AUs. AU ideas are bounced off each other, applied to each other, and combined with each other, in such a way that there are layers upon layers of fan-made content.

The Undertale Multiverse

…What if there was a story in which all of these alternate universes existed together in some sort of hellish abomination reminiscent of the movie Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse but every character was instead an alternate version of Sans from Undertale?

The fandom began becoming meta with the appearance of the character Error!Sans, who canonically destroyed alternate universes, and the character Ink!Sans, who sort of served as Error's counterpart, who canonically defended the AUs.

The universe of Dreamtale is a particular one, being that despite its name implying it to be an AU of Undertale, it is a unique story that takes place in this fan-made multiverse.

And Underverse, the sequel to Jael Peñaloza's XTale AU, is a sort of crossover that features a lot of different characters from different AUs.

List of Undertale AUs

There are countless Undertale AUs, so I cannot list all of them, But I can list some of the most popular and/or well known so you'll have a place to start.

Underfell, the first Undertale AU ever created, in which all of the characters are edgy. Unfortunately, its creator, Vic the Underfella, has left the platform and the original material for this AU has been lost to time.

Flowerfell, an AU of Underfell in which Frisk grows a flower somewhere on their body each time they die.

Underswap, an AU created by PopcornPr1nce who has since left the platform, in which the personalities and roles of Sans and Papyrus, Undyne and Alphys, Frisk and Chara, Toriel and Asgore, and a lot of other characters are swapped with each other.

Fellswap Gold, Fellswap Red, Fellswap Emerald, and Swapfell which are all to my understanding some sort of combination of Underfell and Underswap.

Dusttale, an AU in which Murder!Sans, its version of Sans, decides to kill all his friends in an attempt to gain the LV needed to defeat the human after witnessing everyone's repeated genocide.

Something New, (in which its creator, Rahafwabas, was the only person on this list with the brainpower necessary to create an actual name for their AU instead of basing the name on Undertale,) in which Killer!Sans, its version of Sans, becomes apathetic and empty inside, and strikes a deal with the human, agreeing to help them commit genocide, out of boredom and a desire to feel emotions once more. He canonically ends up working for Nightmare!Sans from Dreamtale.

Reapertale, in which everyone is a god and Sans is the grim reaper.

Dreamtale, which has lore too complicated to summarize into a few sentences.

XTale, in which Cross!Sans, its version of Sans, is endlessly tormented by XGaster, an angry purple man who basically acts like Gabriel Agreste from Miraculous Ladybug but less cringy, combined with Darkstalker from Wings of Fire who can basically edit reality.

Underlust, in which you should get the heck out of here if you're under 18 years old, which is actually more mature and has deeper and darker lore than most people are aware of.

tl;dr basically Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse but everything is Sans the Skeleton from Undertale
